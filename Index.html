Do you own a laptop?

Laptops are portable, compact computers designed for personal and professional use. They typically consist of a keyboard, display screen, and internal components
such as a processor, memory, storage, and various ports for connectivity. Laptops come in various sizes, ranging from ultraportable notebooks to larger gaming or
workstation models. They can run different operating systems, such as Windows, macOS, or Linux, and are versatile devices used for tasks like productivity, internet
browsing, content creation, gaming, and more. Factors like processing power, RAM, storage capacity, graphics capabilities, and battery life vary across laptop models,
catering to different user needs and preferences.

Do you own a Desktop?

Desktop computers are fixed computing systems comprising a monitor, keyboard, mouse, and processing unit. They vary in configurations tailored to specific needs,
from basic setups for everyday tasks to high-performance rigs for gaming, design, or scientific computing. Key components include the CPU, RAM, storage drives,
GPU, and motherboard. They offer advantages like upgradability, customization, and superior performance over laptops or tablets. While lacking portability, desktops
are preferred for their robustness and versatility, finding applications in offices, homes, and industries. Despite the rise of portable devices, desktops continue to evolve
with compact designs, improved energy efficiency, and enhanced connectivity, maintaining their relevance in the computing landscape.
